Welcome to the inaugural season of Fashion Futures, a captivating exploration of creativity, diversity, and the challenges shaping the fashion industry in Southeast Asia.

This innovative series is proudly presented by the Singapore Fashion Council in partnership with ASN Media.

Fashion Futures features exclusive interviews with industry leaders, in-depth reports, and profiles of pioneering organisations driving circularity and sustainability.

/01 Cultivate

Explores the seeds of circularity, looking at the generation of new materials, sustainable farming, and raw materials that feed into the circular ecosystem.


Olam Agri

From Australia to the Ivory Coast, Olam Agri partners with farmers and processors to minimise chemical and water use, maximise organic inputs, and support farmers' communities, connecting with brands and retailers on ethical and sustainability preferences.
CEO Insights

Singapore Fashion Council CEO Zhang Ting Ting discusses the importance of greener principles in cultivating raw materials and considers end-of-life aspects for a sustainable fashion future.
Oritain

Using advanced forensic technology to combat fibre fraud, Oritain provides visibility for customers, enabling them to verify the origins of their purchased goods.
Xefco

Innovative technology from Geelong, Australia, Xefco's Ausora plasma coating process aims to reduce water pollution from textile processing, potentially revolutionising textile dyeing and finishing.

/02 Create

Highlights how circularity is designed through product development, be it artisanship, digital solutions in the supply chain, zero-waste concepts, and social responsibilities.


Browzwear

Advancing digital design with 3D concepts, Browzwear is driving towards a more eco-friendly fashion future without compromising profitability.
CEO Insights

Zhang Ting Ting, CEO of Singapore Fashion Council, talks about empowering designers and procurement officers to prioritise zero-waste designs with minimal environmental impact.
H&M

Tracking the journey of a fabric piece from a Vietnamese factory to a Singapore store, H&M leads in circular and climate-positive fashion initiatives.
Raffles Design

Raffles Design is pushing the boundaries of sustainability and creativity, aligning with the industry's move towards ecologically responsible practices.
Kathmandu

Pioneering sustainable outdoor fashion, Kathmandu transforms waste into performance gear, supports design longevity through repair programmes, and is a certified B Corp committed to positive environmental impact.

/03 Use

Examines alternative business models in retail and how consumption, regulated by policies/standards or social responsibility, contributes to circularity.


Be the Change Summit

An annual event by the Singapore Fashion Council that brings together key voices in the fashion industry, sharing insights for those committed to a sustainable future.
CEO Insights

Zhang Ting Ting discusses 'conscious consumption' and envisions a future where the industry educates consumers on ethical and environmental choices.

/04 Rescue

Looks at the End of Life of products as well as the processes and technologies that are working to bring the value chain towards a closed loop model.


Eternally Circular Gala

A glittering annual event hosted by the Singapore Fashion Council, celebrating circularity and creativity with some of the fashion world's most influential figures.

Rescue - Industry Perspective

Industry leaders discuss textile-to-textile recycling and urban fit technology investments in Singapore, featuring insights from Zhang Ting Ting, J J Eastwood, Luke Henning, Rena Lim, and Professor Hu Xiao.
